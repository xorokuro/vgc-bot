📐 How Damage Is Calculated

Every damage roll in Gen 9 follows the same formula. Once you internalize each piece, you can estimate ranges in your head.

Damage = ⌊ ⌊ 22 × Atk × BP / Def ⌋ / 50 ⌋ + 2   ×   Modifiers

The 22 comes from the level-50 factor: ⌊(2×50/5) + 2⌋ = 22

1
Calculate the attacker's effective stat
Start from the base stat, add EV/IV contributions, apply nature, then apply any in-battle boosts (+1 to +6 stages multiply by 1.5×, 2×, 2.5×, 3×, 3.5×, 4×).
2
Calculate the defender's effective stat
Same process. Wonder Room swaps Def ↔ SpD before this step. Unaware ignores the opponent's boosts.
3
Compute base damage
base = ⌊ ⌊22 × Atk × BP / Def⌋ / 50 ⌋ + 2
4
Apply pre-roll modifiers
Spread (×0.75 in doubles), Weather boost/weaken (×1.5 / ×0.5), Critical hit (×1.5).
5
Apply the random roll
Multiply by one of 16 values from 85/100 to 100/100. This is why you always see a range of 16 different damage values.
6
Apply post-roll modifiers
STAB (×1.5), Type effectiveness (×0.5 / ×2 / ×4), Burn (×0.5), Screens (÷2 singles, ×2732/4096 doubles), Friend Guard (×0.75).

Stat Formula at Level 50

HP Stat
⌊ (2×Base + IV + ⌊EV÷4⌋) × 0.5 ⌋ + 60
Other Stats (Atk / Def / SpA / SpD / Spe)
⌊ ( ⌊ (2×Base + IV + ⌊EV÷4⌋) × 0.5 ⌋ + 5 ) × Nature ⌋

Nature is 1.1 (beneficial), 0.9 (detrimental), or 1.0 (neutral). Max EVs = 252, max IVs = 31.

HP is never affected by nature. The constant 76 = 60 (level anchor) + 16 (31 IVs ÷ 2 rounded).

Why +20 and +76?

At Level 50 with 31 IVs and 0 EVs, the inner part of the stat formula is:

Non-HP: ⌊ (2×base + 31) × 0.5 ⌋ + 5 ≈ base + 15.5 + 5 = 20.5 → ~20
HP:     ⌊ (2×base + 31) × 0.5 ⌋ + 60 ≈ base + 15.5 + 60 = 75.5 → ~76

Every 8 EVs adds exactly 1 to the final stat (because floor(EVs/4) × 0.5 = EVs/8). So 252 EVs adds exactly 31 to the raw value, which we round to +32 for quick mental math.

Applying Nature in Your Head

+10% nature (beneficial):

  • Divide the stat by 10, then add it.
    e.g. stat = 182 → 182÷10 ≈ 18 → 182+18 = 200
  • Or: multiply by 1.1 by doing stat × 1.1
    182 × 1.1 = 200.2 → 200

−10% nature (detrimental):

  • Divide the stat by 10, then subtract it.
    e.g. stat = 182 → 182÷10 ≈ 18 → 182−18 = 164
  • Or: multiply by 0.9.
    182 × 0.9 = 163.8 → 163

🔢 Modifier Reference

Each modifier is multiplied into the damage in order. They stack multiplicatively — STAB + 2× type = ×3 total damage.

STAB
×1.5

Same Type Attack Bonus. Adaptability makes it ×2. Tera STAB on a non-native type is also ×1.5 (×2 if it matches the original type too).

Type Effectiveness
×0.25 / ×0.5 / ×2 / ×4

Stacked per type interaction. Double-weak (×4) Pokémon are extremely vulnerable — a STAB super-effective hit is ×6.

Weather Boost/Weaken
×1.5 / ×0.5

Sun boosts Fire, weakens Water. Rain boosts Water, weakens Fire. Air Lock / Cloud Nine nullifies weather effects on damage.

Terrain Boost
×1.3

Electric/Grassy/Psychic Terrain boost their respective types for grounded Pokémon. Misty Terrain halves Dragon moves.

Spread Move (Doubles)
×0.75

Any move that hits both opponents (Earthquake, Heat Wave, etc.) in Doubles does 75% damage. Singles is unaffected.

Reflect / Light Screen
×0.5 singles / ×0.667 doubles

Reflect halves physical damage, Light Screen halves special. In Doubles the reduction is ×2732/4096 ≈ ⅔ instead of ½. Aurora Veil applies the same multiplier as a screen for that category.

Critical Hit
×1.5

Also ignores negative attacker stat drops and positive defender stat boosts. Does not ignore screens.

Burn
×0.5 (physical)

Halves the damage of physical moves. Guts ignores the damage penalty. Does not affect special moves.

Friend Guard
×0.75

Doubles-only. An ally with Friend Guard reduces all damage taken by any teammate by 25%.

Stat Boosts (Stages)
+1→×1.5, +2→×2, +6→×4

Every +1 stage adds 0.5 to the multiplier. −1 = ×0.667, −2 = ×0.5, −6 = ×0.25. Stored Power gains 20 BP per positive stage.

Wonder Room
Def ↔ SpD swap

All Pokémon use their SpD in place of Def (and vice-versa) for damage calculation. Great for punishing high-SpD defensive Pokémon with physical moves.

Magic Room
Items suppressed

Held items have no effect. Life Orb, Choice Band, Eviolite, Assault Vest — all ignored for 5 turns.

🧠 Mental Math Shortcuts

Experienced players don't calculate exactly — they use benchmarks and rough multipliers to estimate damage in seconds.

  • The ÷2 Rule for Equal Stats:
    If attacker's effective Attack equals defender's effective Defense, a 100 BP move at Level 50 does roughly 44 base damage before modifiers (22×100÷100÷50+2 = 44). Use 44 as your anchor.
  • Rough Damage %:
    Damage% ≈ (Atk × BP) / (Def × HP / 100 × 2.3)
    This over-simplifies the formula but gives you a ballpark within ~5%. For quick mental checks, just ask: "is the attacker's Atk×BP more or less than 2.3× the defender's Def×HP/100?"
  • STAB + Super-Effective = ×3:
    A STAB move into a 2× weakness triples the damage. If the base deal was 25%, it becomes ~75%. STAB + 4× weakness is ×6 — almost always an OHKO.
  • EV Investment Impact:
    Going from 0 EVs → 252 EVs in an offensive stat adds roughly +63 to the inner formula, which translates to about +15–20% more stat. A Choice Band (+50%) is far stronger than EVs alone.
  • +1 Stage is Huge:
    A +1 Attack boost multiplies your damage by ×1.5 — equivalent to adding 50% more base power to every move. +2 is ×2. Getting a Dragon Dance or Swords Dance off usually means the next hit kills something it couldn't before.
  • Doubles Spread = Singles ×0.75:
    Your Earthquake that does 60% in singles only does 45% in doubles. This is why single-target coverage moves (Close Combat, Iron Head) are often preferred in VGC over spread moves.
  • Screens Make OHKOs into 2HKOs:
    In doubles, a screen reduces damage by ~33%. A move that would OHKO without a screen might only do ~67% with one, turning a clean KO into a 2HKO. Factor this into your win condition timing.
  • The 16-Roll Range:
    The minimum roll is always 85% of the maximum roll. So if the max is 100 damage, the min is 85. The range you see is min–max across those 16 values. In practice, the middle values are each equally likely, so focus on whether the minimum guarantees a KO.
  • Common Defensive Benchmarks to Memorize:
    • 252 HP Incineroar at Lv50 has 197 HP
    • 252 HP Rillaboom at Lv50 has 207 HP
    • 252 HP Flutter Mane at Lv50 has 139 HP
    • Max SpD Assault Vest Incineroar tanks special hits extremely well (~90 SpD)
    Once you know these HP values, you can quickly judge whether a damage range crosses the KO threshold.
